ABOUT US

Who we are

We are a team of web developers and humanitarian activists that believe simple tools and the right support and training can help charitable organisations spend less time wrangling with technology and more time focusing on their cause. We have worked with JustGiving, Google, Sainsbury's, Harvard Business School, Ushahidi, DOT and many others.

What we're doing

Through partnerships with NGOs and other actors in humanitarian aid, we will build, deploy and support SMS­- and web-­based applications that meet the needs of those adversely affected by conflict and those who seek to help these people. We are actively seeking out opportunities for partnerships in Beirut and Istanbul.

Why we're doing it

We are a not-for-profit organisation based in London, staffed by volunteers who believe that technology and the information it delivers can empower people to improve the quality of their lives.

This is not a new idea.
We simply offer our digital know-how in order to catalyse a process that is already happening.

HISTORY

A hackathon in London

June 2013: Facilitated by Chayn and supported by the Embassy of Sweden, the Embassy of the Kingdom of Netherlands and the UK Foreign and Commonwealth Office, The Global Summit on Sexual Violence in Conflict hosted a hackathon with the aim of creating technical solutions to the problems faced by survivors of sexual violence in conflict. Read more about it here.

We won!

Our app, "The Promise", won first prize. That weekend, we built an app to connect survivors of sexual violence via SMS and voice to the appropriate humanitarian services.

What happened next

We went back to the NGOs who advised us during the competition and asked them about the challenges they faced. We spoke to organisations focused on gender-based violence in conflict. We interviewed companies who build technology for development.

We found that there are a great number of humanitarian-focused digital tools that already exist of which NGOs are not aware. We found that there is a dearth of coders available to build new tools. We realised that there is an abundance of coders based in London who are keen to help.

Our aim is to connect NGOs that help those adversely affected by conflict with technologists who want to build digital tools for social good.
