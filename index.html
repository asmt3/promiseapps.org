ABOUT US

Who we are

We are a team of web developers and humanitarian activists that believe simple tools and the right support and training can help charitable organisations spend less time wrangling with technology and more time focusing on their cause. We have worked with JustGiving, Google, Sainsbury's, Harvard Business School, Ushahidi, DOT and many others.

What we're doing

Through partnerships with NGOs and other actors in humanitarian aid, we will build, deploy and support SMS­- and web-­based applications that meet the needs of those adversely affected by conflict and those who seek to help these people. We are actively seeking out opportunities for partnerships with NGOs and technologists based in Beirut and Istanbul.

Why we're doing it

We are a not-for-profit organisation based in London, staffed by volunteers who believe that technology and the information it delivers can empower people to improve the quality of their lives. Most of all, we like building useful digital tools and we're excited about the opportunity to do this in a humanitarian context.

HISTORY

A hackathon in London

June 2014: Facilitated by Chayn and supported by the Embassy of Sweden, the Embassy of the Kingdom of Netherlands and the UK Foreign and Commonwealth Office, The Global Summit on Sexual Violence in Conflict hosted a hackathon with the aim of creating technical solutions to the problems faced by survivors of sexual violence in conflict. Read more about it here.

We won!

Our app, "The Promise", won first prize. That weekend, we built an app to connect survivors of sexual violence via SMS and voice to the appropriate humanitarian services.

What happened next

We went back to the NGOs who advised us during the competition and asked them about the challenges they faced. We spoke to organisations focused on gender-based violence in conflict. We interviewed companies who build technology for development.

We found that there are a great number of humanitarian-focused digital tools that already exist of which some NGOs were not aware. We established that some of the technical requirements presented to us by NGOs would be straight forward to meet. We realised that there is an abundance of coders based in London who are keen to help.

Our aim is to connect NGOs that help those adversely affected by conflict with technologists who want to build digital tools for social good.
